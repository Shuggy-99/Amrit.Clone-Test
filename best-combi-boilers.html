What is a combi boiler?

A combi or combination boiler combines a water heating system and central heating unit into a single body, acting as a unified way to handle heating and hot water without needing two separate boilers.

This means there is no need for a separate water tank system and therefore takes up less space in your home. A combi boiler is one of the most efficient types of combi boilers to install and tends to be much more reliable than normal combi boilers which is why we love them so much!

Combi boilers are suitable for most properties

A combi boiler is the best option for almost all properties because it can be easily installed onto the water mains and offer instant control of heating in your home.

Whether your current combi boiler is on the blink or not, all combi boilers need to be replaced every 10 to 15 years. This means that even if you are not currently experiencing problems with your old combi boiler, but it has been in your home for more than a decade, it is time to consider what combi boiler can replace it.

Why should I buy a new boiler?

According to the Energy Saving Trust, if your combi boiler is over fifteen years old and is a non-condensing boiler, it will be less efficient and may become faulty and even break down on a regular basis, as a result of corrosion on internal components from the thermal and mechanical stresses over years of constant use.

Not only is this an inconvenience for you, but it could also be costing you a lot more money to run an old inefficient boiler that frequently requires costly repairs, than it would to run a new A-Class efficiency rated combi boiler.

According to The Energy Saving Trust, you could start saving up to £340 annually on your energy bills by replacing your combi boiler

Energy efficiency of your combi boiler

Combi boilers are given a grade based on how energy-efficient they are, with an A rating being the highest.

This is a good thing to consider because it will determine how expensive the boiler will be to run. A more energy-efficient boiler could potentially save you money over time.

Power outage of the combi boiler

The power outage of a combi boiler is listed as kW, and this determines the size of the boiler. Smaller homes tend to benefit from a boiler of between 25kW and 30kW, but this is something your gas engineer can help you with.

Size of the combi boiler

You will need a combi boiler that is large enough to handle your home heating.

The physical size of a combi boiler unit is important to consider too, as this will determine whether the unit can be installed into your home safely.

How much does a new combi boiler cost?

The average cost of getting a new combi boiler is between £1,500 and £3,000.

However the cost of a boiler can vary depending on the brand and the type.

When you get a new combi boiler, there are various costs involved that you need to consider along with the price of the unit itself.

Buying a boiler includes installation costs, labour costs, and potentially any additional parts as well as the price of the boiler itself.

Different gas companies offer varying prices based on boiler location, availability, and your location.

For example, those in London will have to pay higher costs than those in the North even if they are getting the exact same combi boiler installed.

The type of home that you live in can also alter the cost of getting a new combi boiler, such as whether you live in a terraced or semi-detached property.

Boiler engineers will also need to consider how many bedrooms and bathrooms are in your home to gauge not only the kind of combi boiler you need but also the cost of installation.

Which combi boiler do I choose?

The problem with choosing the best combi boilers is that every homeowner can want different things.

Combi boilers are meant to heat water and provide heating, so it is obvious that they will need to do those basic things, but other features can really vary depending on what you are looking for.

For example, some people are desperate for high-efficiency models that cost less to run, especially if they are already on a tight budget and do not want high heating bills that can eat into their savings.

Other people have more than enough money to cover the bills and want higher heating speeds or temperatures instead.

On top of that, there are some people who just want the lowest install price and do not care about the actual effectiveness of the boiler, especially in rental properties or homes that they are renovating. In many cases, a cheap boiler can be good holdover until you are ready to replace it with something more expensive.

Price of a new combi boiler

If the price is all you care about, then the best combi boilers are the ones that do not cost much to install and cost even less to run. We do also have an in-depth blog on combi boiler costs to help you better decide when purchasing a new boiler.

A cheap but inefficient boiler will add up over time, so something that is efficient and low-priced can be the best choice in terms of affordability.

If you can get the boiler on a finance plan rather than with an upfront payment, this becomes even better.

Remember that installation fees are sometimes a thing, so you’ll want to keep that in mind if you are not going to install the combi-boiler yourself.

The more complex the process, the more it can cost, especially if you need new pipes and systems to be installed so that the boiler can connect properly.

Size of the boiler

A bigger gas combi boiler can often be more powerful, but it also takes up more room and will generally make more noise.

If a boiler is too bulky, then you will need space to keep it somewhere, and that can often mean that you will have to do more work opening up space in a wall or building something to cover it.

On the other hand, a compact boiler can easily be fitted into existing spaces with minimal effort, and usually sounds quieter.

On the other hand, this might also reduce the power it uses and the level of heating that it can offer: improving efficiency but reducing overall strength.

Neither is necessarily better than the other, it just comes down to personal preference and the number of items in your home that will need hot water or higher water pressure to operate.

Quality of the boiler

Many of the best combi's will be the ones built to a high standard of quality. There are rarely any times where a low-quality boiler is better unless it is far cheaper, and in most cases, a single high-quality combi boiler can cost less than having to replace a low-quality option multiple times.

If you have the option, always go for the better-quality models if they are available, and be sure to look up any common issues with the boiler before you decide to purchase it.

Warranties can matter a lot, too, since they will sometimes make up for a drop in quality by allowing you to get a replacement or repair job done.

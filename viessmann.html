Viessmann Combi Boilers

If you are considering replacing your older boiler with a combi, or looking for a like-for-like swap, Viessmann is a brand you should consider.

We understand the different types of boilers available can be confusing, so here is a quick summary on what a Combi Boiler is (excerpt taken from our full guide - What Is A Combi Boiler? ):

Combi boilers, (short for 'combination boiler') are a type of boiler that combines a water heating system and a central heating unit into a single body, acting as a unified way to handle heating and hot water without needing two separate boilers.

Our entire combi boiler range is rated energy efficiency class: A. All of the boilers we provide are built around our long established reputation for high quality heating systems and are equipped with the latest heating technology.”

Viessmann Heat Only (Regular Boilers)

Another option you have when purchasing a new boiler, is a heat only boiler. Heat only boilers, commonly referred to as regular boilers, provide the heat that is then used within your home’s central heating system. Hot water is stored in large water tanks rather than shared heating and hot water. Regular boilers are more commonly found in bigger properties.

Open vent boiler systems provide both hot water and heating to your home/premises. An open vent system is usually made up of two water tanks in the loft space, a hot water storage cylinder and a separate pump. As aforementioned, they are usually a good solution for households with multiple bathrooms as they are capable of supplying a lot of hot water at once.

Viessmann System Boilers

Does your home have more than one bathroom? If you have a high demand for hot water, Viessmann’s range of system boilers may be the perfect choice for you. System boilers provide high pressure hot water to multiple taps & showers across a property.

It is worth noting that system boilers feature a storage tank, which will take up some space within the property. System boilers give you a greater hot water capacity than combi boilers.
